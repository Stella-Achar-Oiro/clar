# Understanding Your Results

## Verdict banner

At the top of the results page, CLAR shows an overall verdict for your report. There are four levels:

| Level | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Normal** | All findings are within expected ranges |
| **Watch** | One or more findings are mildly outside range; worth monitoring |
| **Concern** | One or more findings warrant a prompt conversation with your doctor |
| **Urgent** | One or more findings may need timely medical attention |

The verdict is a summary signal, not a diagnosis. Your doctor has your full clinical history and is the right person to interpret these results in context.

## Finding cards

Each finding card contains:

- **Name** — the test or observation as it appeared in your report
- **Plain explanation** — what this finding means in everyday language
- **Your value** — the measured result
- **Reference range** — the typical expected range for this test
- **Urgency level** — Normal, Watch, Concern, or Urgent

## Urgency Summary view

Switch to **Urgency Summary** in the sidebar to see only findings that CLAR flagged as Watch, Concern, or Urgent. This is a useful starting point for deciding what to prioritise before a doctor's appointment.

## Doctor Questions view

CLAR generates a short list of suggested questions based on your specific results. These are designed to help you have a more productive conversation with your clinician — not to replace that conversation.
